Capacitors

Effect of Area and Plate Separation

Increasing the area or using a dielectric will increase the capacitance

Increasing the distance between the plates will decrease the capacitance

Overall we get \( C = \frac{ ( \epsilon_{R} \epsilon_{0} A ) }{ d } \)

The Electric Field

The molecules of a dielectric are usually polar

In an electric field they rotate to line up

Since the charges are in the opposite direction to the original field they will decrease the size of the electric field leading to decreased voltage and increased capacitance.

The electric field is given by \( E = \frac{V}{d} \) \( C = \frac{Q}{V} \)

The addition of a dielectric decreases \( E \) (and \( V \)) by a factor \( \epsilon_{R} - \text{ } \)
