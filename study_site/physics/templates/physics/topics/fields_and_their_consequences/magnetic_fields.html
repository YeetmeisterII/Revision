Magnetic Fields

Fleming's Left Hand Rule

\( F = I \textbf{L} \times \textbf{B} = iLBsin(\theta) \text{ or } F = q \textbf{v} \times \textbf{B} = qvBsin(\theta) \)

  • A wire of length \( L \) carrying a current \( I \) in a magnetic field of stringth \( B \) will experience a force.
  • The force is greatest when the wire is perpendicular to the magnetic field
