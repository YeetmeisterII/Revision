Circular Motion

To have circular motion an object must have a force.

Describing Acceleration in a Circle

Define the circle by the parametric equations \(s_x = rcos(wt)\) & \(s_y = rsin(wt)\)

\(\Rightarrow \frac{ds_x}{dt} = -rwsin(wt)\)

\(\Rightarrow \frac{ds_y}{dt} = rwcos(wt)\)

\(\Rightarrow \frac{d^2 s_x}{dt^2} = -r w^2 cos(wt) = a_x\)

\(\Rightarrow \frac{d^2 s_y}{dt^2} = -r w^2 sin(wt) = a_y\)

\(a_t = \sqrt{a^2_x + a^2_y}\)

\(\Rightarrow a_t = \sqrt{(-r w^2 cos(wt))^2 + (-r w^2 sin(wt))^2}\)

\(\Rightarrow a_t = \sqrt{r^2 w^4 cos^2(wt) + r^2 w^4 sin^2(wt)}\)

\(\Rightarrow a_t = \sqrt{r^2 w^4(cos^2(wt) + sin^2(wt))}\)

\(\Rightarrow a_t = \sqrt{r^2 w^4(1)}\)

\(\Rightarrow a_t = r w^2\)
