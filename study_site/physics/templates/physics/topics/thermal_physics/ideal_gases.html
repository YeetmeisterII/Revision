Ideal Gases

What is an Ideal Gas?

There are no forces between the molecules so the potential energy of the gas is zero.

Any collisions are elastic (Newtonian).

This means the internal energy is only the kinetic energy of the molecules.

Particle in a Box

A particle with mass \(m\) moving with velocity \(C_X\) bouncing perpendicular to the wall of a cube with length \(L\) it's frequency upon that wall is \(\frac{C_X}{2L}\)

The momentum change for one collision will be \(\Delta p_x = 2 m C_x\)

\(\Rightarrow \Delta p_{TOTAL} = 2 m C_x \times \frac{C_x}{2L}\)

\(\Rightarrow \Delta p_{TOTAL} = \frac{m C_x^2}{L}\)

Generalise

The box has \(N\) molecules.

The molecules can move equally in the \(X, Y, Z\) directions

\(\Rightarrow C_x = C_y = C_z\)

For N molecules in 3 dimensions

\(F = \frac{1}{3} \frac{N m C^2}{L}\)

In the cube with lengths \(L\) we can ascertain pressure to be

\(P = \frac{F}{A} = \frac{1}{3} \frac{N m C^2}{L} \div L^2 = \frac{1}{3} \frac{N m C^2}{L^3}\)

\(V = L^3\), where V is the volume of the cube therefore we can simplify the problem

\(P = \frac{1}{3} \frac{N m C^2}{V}\)

We can put this into the more useful form \(PV = \frac{1}{3} N m C^2\)
