Thinking Abstractly

What is abstraction?

Existing in thought or as an idea but not having a physical or concrete existence.

Abstraction in Computer Programming

Taking a complex idea or object and removing unnecessary data to create a model of it on a computer.

The Nature of Abstraction

We build "model" of the real item, but we only reproduce the relevant features of those objects to reduce the amount of processing required.

Why We Have Abstraction

Allows for complex procedures or events to be modelled

An exact representation of real life would be too complex for a program to manage.
